About Me

Hello! I'm Abigail Bahiru, an Ethiopian student studying Interactive Design at Kennesaw State University, graduating December 2025. My passion is creating **digital experiences that are both functional and emotionally engaging**.

During my time at KSU, I worked on **interactive projects and mobile apps**, including collaborative design projects, usability research, and motion design prototypes. I enjoy combining creativity with technology to solve real-world problems.

Outside of design, I enjoy **sketching, photography, exploring UX trends, and learning about cultural storytelling**. My Ethiopian heritage inspires my appreciation for vibrant visuals, storytelling, and user-centered design approaches.

Portfolio

Food App: RecipeShare

RecipeShare is a social mobile app where users can **share, remix, and discover recipes**. I focused on creating a user-friendly and visually appealing interface that encourages exploration and community engagement.

Features & Highlights:

Owl Park: Campus Parking App

Owl Park is an interactive map app for Kennesaw State University, displaying **real-time parking availability** to help students plan campus visits efficiently.

Features & Highlights:

  • Designed motion prototypes with smooth transitions between map and detail screens.
  • Implemented accessibility-focused visual design for all users.
  • Optimized UX hierarchy to reduce student stress and confusion.

My Process

Discovery: Conduct research, inspiration boards, and user interviews to uncover insights.
Define: Map user journeys, define problems, and find opportunities to improve the experience.
Design: Create wireframes, prototypes, and visual designs with accessibility and usability in mind.
Test & Iterate: Gather feedback and perform usability testing to refine solutions.
Delivery: Prepare final assets, documentation, and handoff for development or deployment.
